Hi I am Nivedita. I love coding and problem solving regardless of the platform or technology in use. I am currently learning Web development, and having fun building projects using React and Node.js.
I moved to Finland in 2015. Before moving to Finland, I worked in application development for 7 years in HP Nonstop mainframe in Atos and Tech Mahindra.
I"ve worked on SQL, HP COBOL, TACL, pathway management among other languages.


I have a Bachelor"s in Electrical engineering. I recently completed a full stack training course at The Shortcut. I am doing the fullstack open course offered by the University of Helsinki. I have completed a web development bootcamp course from udemy. A few years ago I had done a course in Salesforce and completed the Salesforce developer certification(expired).

I am self-starter. At the beginning of my career in IT, I had to work on a very rare technology (HP Nonstop) with practically no training and no support. I had to learn a lot and do it very quickly. It was very challenging and required a lot of effort, help was not easily found on online forums. However, it shaped me as developer and helped me gain a lot of confidence in my learning and problem solving abilities. My problems as a trainee also made me empathise with other developers and I always made sure to share as much knowledge as I could with my team members and always be there to help.

I speak a moderate amount of Finnish. I have a B2.1 YKI certificate. I speak four other languages Hindi, Konkani, Marathi and to state the obvious English.



I love reading, topics like evolution, environment and natural history interest me. Growing up I read a lot of fiction, now I occasional listen to fictional audiobooks in Finnish to help improve my language skills. Currently, I am listening to "Yksi elämä, yksi pläneetta" by David Attenborough (non-fiction) and reading "Of Women" by Shami Chakrabarti. Bird watching also interests me. One of my new found hobbies is cycling, due to the pandemic and to avoid public transport, I have started cycling again after 20 years. So I’m pretty adaptable that way.
